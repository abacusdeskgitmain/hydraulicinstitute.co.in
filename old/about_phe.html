About PHE


PHE Institute Of Hydraulics (PHE) was established in the year 2010 with the objective of providing modern education with special emphasis on Technical courses, Information Technology and Management through virtual and distance learning modes, management and development of Technical related services and working towards overall development of the society, specially for the benefit of under privileged groups.

PHE Institute Of Hydraulics (PHE) is an autonomous organization aims to spread knowledge with global standard education system and help explore one's creativity, so that every student contributes to the social and national growth. At PHE, every student is equally important. They have expert faculties dedicated to help students at every instant and ensure that they participate in a dynamic learning process. PHE offers wide range of programmes that refine knowledge and propel personal and professional growth
.
PHE is established with VISION 2020 MISSION of Dr. A P J Abdul Kalam and provide employable skill-based education for the task of knowledge acquisition, knowledge imparting, knowledge creation and knowledge sharing. PHE, has taken his words as direction towards the future and share the responsibility in transforming India with skilled youth by 2020.

This PHE Institute of Hydraulics is an initiative of Precision Hydraulic Engineers Faridabad this company was established in August’ 2000 as a proprietorship company, keeping in mind the vast potential existing in after sales support of Heavy Earthmoving & construction equipment. Our main area of activity is to provide cost effective and durable repair of hydraulic components fitted on these machines. We also supply genuine parts but at a lower cost than the OEM’s thanks to our knowledge and experience in the said field.

Precision Hydraulic Engineers is a professionally managed company with interests in after sales support of Hydraulic Road Construction and Concrete equipment.
Our aim is to provide our customer reliable and cheaper maintenance options in order to reduce overall operating cost of the machine.
